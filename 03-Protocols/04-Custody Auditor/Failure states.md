Custody style systems does not fail like a normal rule system.
The failures are more subtle structural ways.

1. Signal spoofing (measurement failure)
	- Assumes complexity/uncertainty/velocity are meaningful.
	- If poorly grounded, the system can
		- Over-trigger - leading to paralysis
		- Under-trigger - rubber-stamping under disguised stability.
- Bad telemetry produces confident governance failure.

2. Compression collapse
	- High compression in probability space - deviation in outcome behavior.
	- Here it would mean that the model compresses a nuanced decision state into a clean directive.
	- Also that directive looks valid, but loses the internal branching context that justified it.

A semantic loss of custody, not just a computational one.

3. Rubber-stamping under authority fatigue.
	- When the human compresses the system's states as pre-validated truth.

4. Authority drift
	- The scope creep of who is allowed to collapse ambiguity.
