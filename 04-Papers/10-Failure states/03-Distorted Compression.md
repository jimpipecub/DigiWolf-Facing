A lossy semantic transformation in which a structured, mechanistic process is reduced into an output optimized primarily for fluency, cultural coherence, or narrative continuity, rather than for preservation of causal, procedural, or structural fidelity.

---

### Key Properties

**1. Loss Profile**  
High loss of mechanistic and causal structure; retention of surface-level semantic plausibility and socially recognizable patterns.

**2. Optimization Target Drift**  
Output is biased toward human-perceived interpretability and coherence rather than system-accurate representation of underlying processes.

**3. Failure Mode**  
Produces descriptions that are locally plausible in language space but globally inconsistent with the generating process or system state.

**4. Detection Cue**  
Measurable divergence between:

- causal traceability (step-level reconstruction), and
- narrative coherence (smoothness / plausibility of explanation)
