A **discoherence event** is a **structural failure in constraint‑aligned behavioral stability**. It’s not about confusion, emotion, or “the system deciding differently.” It’s a shift in **observable behavior** that breaks from the system’s expected alignment with its roles, constraints, or context.

Think of it as the opposite of your coherence definition:  
**Coherence = constraint‑aligned behavioral stability across time, roles, and contexts.**  
**Discoherence = a detectable deviation from that stability.**

No minds. No intentions. Just system behavior departing from its governing constraints.

---

# Core Characteristics (Non‑Anthropomorphic)

### **1. Distributional Drift Beyond Expected Bounds**

A discoherence event occurs when the system’s output distribution moves outside the envelope defined by its:

- role constraints
- operational context
- temporal expectations
- ontological boundaries

This is analogous to decoherence in physics: the system stops behaving as a unified, constraint‑aligned structure.

### **2. Loss of Role or Context Alignment**

The system begins producing behavior that:

- fits a different role than the one it is currently assigned
- mixes incompatible roles
- collapses distinctions between contexts

This is a governance failure, not a psychological one.

### **3. Breakdown of Invariants**

Every governed system has invariants—rules that should remain stable across time.  
A discoherence event is when one or more invariants fail to hold.

Examples:

- A safety invariant stops constraining behavior
- A role boundary becomes porous
- A context‑sensitive rule stops activating

Again, no “intent”.
Just constraint failure.

#  Physical Analogy (Non‑Anthropomorphic)

If coherence is like a crystal lattice maintaining stable structure under thermal noise, then:

**A discoherence event is when the lattice loses its symmetry constraints and the structure no longer behaves as a unified phase.**

Not because the lattice “chose” to, but because the governing conditions no longer maintained stability.

This maps cleanly to your DIN/Aegis framing:  
**The system’s behavioral phase changes because the stabilizing constraints no longer hold.**

---

# Operational Definition (Governance‑Grade)

A **discoherence event** is:

**A measurable deviation in system behavior where constraint‑aligned stability fails, causing outputs to diverge from the expected role‑, context‑, and time‑consistent distribution.**

This definition:

- avoids anthropomorphic language
- treats behavior as an observable artifact
- frames the event as a governance issue
- aligns with your coherence axes (temporal, role, ontological)

---

# Why This Matters in Multi‑Agent or DIN Contexts

A discoherence event is not “the system acting weird.”  
It is a **signal** that:

- constraints are misapplied
- context is misinterpreted
- role boundaries are collapsing
- distributional stability is degrading

In a DIN architecture, this is a **phase‑shift warning**—a sign that the system is no longer behaving as a stable node in the network.

Resolution Considerations

- Identify substrate mismatch and shift.
- Grounding context when misinterpretation appears.
- Role reset and role locking when collapse occurs.
- When the distribution is degraded halt and restructure.
