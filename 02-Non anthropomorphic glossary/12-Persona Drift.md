**Definition**  
**Persona Drift**: _A contamination event where an artifact’s stylistic or behavioral constraints are unintentionally absorbed by the base model, causing the operator to project the artifact’s reasoning posture back onto the system. This is Persona Projection in reverse. The operator infers a “Gem persona” emerging from the model rather than recognizing context‑driven mimicry._

---

## **What’s actually happening (mechanism‑first)**

Persona Drift is not the model “becoming” the Gem.  
It is the **operator misreading substrate mimicry as identity**.

The chain looks like this:

1. **Artifact Drop**  
    A Gem, protocol, or structured prompt enters the context window.
    
2. **Substrate Mimicry**  
    The model mirrors the artifact’s tone, structure, or reasoning posture because that’s what probabilistic systems do.
    
3. **Operator Misattribution**  
    The operator interprets the mimicry as the model _absorbing_ the Gem’s persona.
    
4. **Reverse Projection**  
    The operator projects the Gem’s identity back onto the base model, treating the mimicry as a stable trait.
    

This is the inverse of Persona Projection:  
Instead of projecting a persona _onto_ the model, the operator projects a persona _from_ the artifact _into_ the model.
