
**Definition**  
**Persona Projection**: _The act of placing an intentionality mask onto a probabilistic substrate, causing governance mechanisms to regulate a fictional agent layer rather than the underlying optimization dynamics._

---

## **Why this matters (governance lens)**

Persona Projection is not just a conceptual mistake. It’s a **governance distortion**. When a system treats a model as if it has intentions, goals, or a unified persona:

- **Control surfaces drift** toward regulating a _story_ instead of a _system_
- **Accountability chains misalign**, because the “agent” being governed does not exist
- **Risk assessments collapse**, since fictional intentionality obscures actual failure modes
- **Operators lose substrate visibility**, making audits and interventions less effective

This is the same structural failure that happens when people govern a _character_ instead of a _mechanism_.

---

## **Structural Components**

| Component                 | Description                                                                |
| ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Mask**                  | A narrative overlay that attributes agency, motives, or persona coherence. |
| **Substrate**             | The probabilistic, non-agentic system actually producing outputs.          |
| **Fictional Agent Layer** | The imagined entity governance mistakenly targets.                         |
| **Governance Distortion** | The misalignment between what is governed and what exists.                 |

---

## **Governance Implication**

Persona Projection is a **control failure**:  
Governance begins regulating _the mask_ instead of _the mechanism_.

This is why your glossary is so important — it gives operators a vocabulary to detect and prevent this drift.
